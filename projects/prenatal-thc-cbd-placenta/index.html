Prenatal THC + CBD Effects on Rat Placental Development

An in vivo study investigating the combined effects of prenatal THC and CBD exposure on placental morphology and vascular development.

Abstract

This project examines the impact of combined prenatal THC and CBD exposure on rat placental development, focusing on morphological and vascular endpoints across placental regions. Using histological staining, imaging, and quantitative analysis, the study integrates experimental biology with computational morphometrics to characterize treatment-specific effects. The work extends prior single-compound studies by evaluating combined exposure using reproducible, quantitative methods.

Experimental design

The study uses a rat model with controlled prenatal exposure to combined THC and CBD at physiologically relevant doses. Placentas are collected at defined gestational timepoints, processed for histological staining, and imaged at high resolution for downstream analysis.

Quantitative methods

Morphometric endpoints include regional area fractions, vascular density estimates, and tissue composition metrics derived from annotated histological images. Analysis integrates manual and semi-automated approaches to ensure reproducibility across samples.

Preliminary observations

Initial analysis suggests region-specific differences in placental morphology following combined cannabinoid exposure. Detailed quantitative results are being compiled for statistical evaluation.

Limitations and next steps

Sample sizes are constrained by the in vivo model. Complete statistical analysis and interpretation are ongoing. The next phase involves finalizing quantitative comparisons and preparing the manuscript.
